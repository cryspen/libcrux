## What was investigated this session

The 4 held reduce functions need their input `b` to be in libcrux's
INTT-Mont form (`v b · R ≡ b_real · 128 (mod q)`).  This input form
comes from `invert_ntt_montgomery`, whose current post is bounds-only
(`is_bounded_poly(3328, future(re))`).

Five paths to close were enumerated, each with documented costs (see
`proofs/proof-style-guide.md` §12):

1. **Full Tier-3 INTT post** — chain 7 per-poly commute lemmas, hit
   the Z3 timeouts agent F encountered on forward layer 2 (>2.7 min
   on a single subquery), days of structural work.
2. **Single-point admit** at the producer post.
3. **Caller-side `assume`s** at the 4 consumer call sites.
4. **Refactor `invert_ntt_montgomery`** to apply `· 1441` internally,
   sacrificing the fusion optimization (~80-160 SIMD ops/call).
5. **Spec extension into Mont form** — wrong direction; canonical
   hacspec is explicitly Mont-free per `specs/ml-kem/src/ntt.rs:5-36`
   (`MONTGOMERY_R = 1`, etc.).

The decision: stop here.  Document the antipattern in
`proofs/proof-style-guide.md` §12.  Hand off.
